This chapter defines a research agenda for model-assisted widget synthesis.

Evaluating quality and consistency requires metrics for suggestion precision, compilation pass rate, and author acceptance. A useful study compares model-assisted proposals with manual authoring to determine whether the assistance improves speed without sacrificing correctness.

Measuring reasoning, planning, and agency versus interpolation is core. The agenda should distinguish proposals that leverage explicit UX3 metadata and tool outputs from those that merely interpolate from examples. One metric is semantic fidelity to the declared schema.

Human-in-the-loop workflows are also a research priority. The paper should explore interfaces for presenting suggestions, capturing feedback, and applying accepted changes while preserving audit trails.

Responsible guardrails are necessary. The compiler should reject suggestions that violate security, accessibility, or policy constraints. This creates a disciplined workflow where model assistance is integrated into the validated build pipeline.
