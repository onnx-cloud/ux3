---

This chapter defines evaluation and validation criteria for model-assisted synthesis.

Acceptance and trust are measured by the speed of review, modification rates, and developer confidence in suggestions. This reflects whether assistance is genuinely useful rather than distracting.

The chapter also compares local deterministic synthesis with cloud-assisted suggestions. Local tooling provides predictable performance and lower privacy risk, while remote models may provide greater generalization.

Case studies evaluate the complete workflow in real UX3 scenarios. The selected cases should document compile-time verification, the fidelity of generated artifacts, and how the developer accepted or refined proposed changes.
