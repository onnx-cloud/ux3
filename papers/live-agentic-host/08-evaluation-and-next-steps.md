chapter: 8
---

This chapter defines evaluation criteria and next steps for the live host concept.

Evaluation metrics include collaboration throughput, proposal acceptance rate, time to goal completion, replay usefulness, steering effectiveness, and the trustworthiness of agent suggestions. The paper also proposes usability studies focused on mixed human-agent workflows, asking participants to complete goals with and without session memory, replay, and typed plan tools.

Next steps include strengthening multi-participant synchronization, enriching the self-knowledge model, and turning more runtime evidence into portable resources that other tools can consume. Another priority is formalizing the relationship between live host state and compile-time artifacts so that goal progress, plan execution, and validation remain aligned.

The chapter concludes that a successful host should make agentic collaboration feel like a natural extension of the development environment rather than a separate advisory layer.
